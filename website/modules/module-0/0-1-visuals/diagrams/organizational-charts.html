SRE Team Structures

Embedded Model

Best for: Companies with 3-10 product teams, strong engineering culture.
Advantages: Deep product knowledge, faster response, shared ownership.
Trade-offs: Requires more SREs, slower knowledge sharing.
Key Interactions: SRE attends team stand-ups, planning, and retros. Works directly with PMs on reliability requirements and with devs on implementation.

Centralized Model

Best for: Companies with 50-500 engineers, standardized tech stack.
Advantages: Deep expertise, consistent practices, smaller team.
Trade-offs: Potential bottleneck, less product context.
Key Interactions: SRE team acts as internal consultants. Engages with product teams for architecture reviews, sets reliability standards, and provides a platform of tools.

Hybrid Model

Best for: Large enterprises with diverse technology portfolios.
Advantages: Combines scale, expertise, and product context.
Evolution Path: Most organizations evolve to this model.
Key Interactions: A central platform team builds tools for embedded SREs. Embedded SREs work with product teams, and the two SRE groups collaborate in a community of practice.
