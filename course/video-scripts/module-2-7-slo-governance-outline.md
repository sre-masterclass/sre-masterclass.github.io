# Module 2.7: SLO Governance & Organizational Maturity - Content Outline

## Learning Objectives
- Understand SLO governance frameworks and organizational maturity models for scaling SRE practices from startup to enterprise environments
- Master practical implementation of cross-team SLO coordination, ownership patterns, and escalation policies that align with organizational structure and business objectives
- Explain complex relationships between SLO governance maturity, organizational change management, and business value demonstration during scaling and incident scenarios
- Apply systematic SLO governance strategy to establish sustainable organizational practices that evolve with company growth and operational complexity

## Template Selection
- **Chosen Template**: Strategic Foundation Template
- **Rationale**: Students need to understand how SLO practices integrate with organizational structure, governance frameworks, and stakeholder management
- **Duration**: 6-10 minutes (strategic workshop focus with collaborative governance design and organizational change management)
- **Chaos Integration**: `deployment-failure.yml` scenario at 4:30 mark to demonstrate governance effectiveness during change-related incidents

## Content Structure

### Introduction: SLO Governance Reality vs Organizational Chaos (60 seconds)
- **Hook**: "SLO governance frameworks that work beautifully in theory often collapse during organizational growth, team restructuring, and high-pressure incident scenarios"
- **Context**: Theoretical governance models vs real-world organizational dynamics and scaling challenges
- **Problem Setup**: Show same SLO violations handled by different organizational maturity levels with varying effectiveness and business impact
- **Learning Objective Preview**: Build understanding of SLO governance evolution and organizational change management for sustainable SRE practices

### Part 1: SLO Governance Framework & Organizational Maturity Models (2-2.5 minutes)
- **Startup SLO Governance**: Informal coordination with direct team communication and shared responsibility models
- **Growth-Stage Governance**: Structured ownership patterns with cross-functional coordination and escalation frameworks
- **Enterprise SLO Governance**: Formal governance committees, standardized processes, and business integration patterns
- **Maturity Assessment Framework**: Organizational readiness evaluation and governance evolution planning for sustainable SRE growth

### Part 2: Cross-Team Coordination & Ownership Patterns (2-2.5 minutes)
- **SLO Ownership Models**: Service-based vs feature-based vs business-journey ownership with accountability frameworks
- **Cross-Team Communication**: Stakeholder alignment patterns, escalation workflows, and business value demonstration strategies
- **Governance Decision-Making**: Committee structures, approval processes, and organizational change management for SLO evolution
- **Conflict Resolution Framework**: Managing competing priorities, resource allocation, and business objective alignment during SLO governance

### Part 3: Deployment Failure Governance Response (1.5-2 minutes)
- **Scenario 1: Startup Response** - Informal coordination with direct communication and shared incident responsibility
- **Scenario 2: Growth-Stage Response** - Structured escalation with defined ownership and cross-team coordination during deployment failure
- **Scenario 3: Enterprise Response** - Formal governance activation with business stakeholder communication and organizational decision-making
- **Governance Effectiveness Assessment**: Measuring organizational response quality, stakeholder communication, and business impact management

### Part 4: Sustainable SLO Governance & Business Integration (1-1.5 minutes)
- **Business Value Demonstration**: ROI calculation frameworks, downtime cost analysis, and executive stakeholder communication strategies
- **Organizational Change Management**: SLO governance evolution with company growth, team restructuring, and operational maturity development
- **Governance Sustainability**: Long-term framework maintenance, continuous improvement processes, and organizational learning integration
- **Next Steps**: Integration with advanced operational practices, organizational scaling, and business alignment strategies
