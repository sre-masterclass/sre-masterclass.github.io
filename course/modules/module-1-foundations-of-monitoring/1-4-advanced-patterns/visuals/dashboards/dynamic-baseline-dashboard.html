Dynamic Baseline Management & Adaptive Thresholds

The Baseline Intelligence Principle

  • Dynamic Baselines: Instead of a single static threshold, the baseline (in blue) is a 30-day weighted average that adapts to the system's evolving performance profile. Notice how it trends upwards, reflecting gradual changes.
  • Pattern-Aware Thresholds: The thresholds (the shaded area) are not fixed. They widen during business hours (9 AM - 5 PM) to accommodate expected load and tighten during off-hours for higher sensitivity. This is context-sensitive alerting.
  • Performance Drift Detection: The slow, steady increase in latency from Day 15 to Day 30 is an example of gradual performance degradation. A static threshold might not have caught this until it was too late.
  • Outlier Exclusion: The sharp spike on Day 25 is a genuine anomaly. The dynamic baseline is calculated to be resistant to such outliers, preventing a single incident from skewing the definition of "normal."
